**Bipedal Locomotion and ZMP**

Bipedal locomotion is the ability to walk on two legs - a defining 
characteristic of humanoid robots. The Zero Moment Point (ZMP) is the 
key concept for stable bipedal walking.

**Key Insights:**
• **Zero Moment Point (ZMP)**: Point where net moment of inertial and 
  gravitational forces is zero; must stay within support polygon for 
  stability; widely used for gait generation and balance control.
• **Center of Mass (CoM)**: Average position of all mass in the robot 
  body; must be controlled to maintain balance.
• **Support Polygon**: The convex hull of all foot contact points with 
  the ground; ZMP must remain inside this area.

### ✅ Question: "What is forward kinematics?"
**Response:**
```
**Forward Kinematics**

Forward kinematics answers: 'Given the joint angles, where is the 
end-effector?'

**Key Insights:**
• **Denavit-Hartenberg (DH) Parameters**: Four parameters per joint 
  (link length, link twist, link offset, joint angle)
• **Transformation Matrices**: 4x4 homogeneous transformation matrix 
  for each joint
• **Chain Rule**: T_total = T1 × T2 × T3 × ... × Tn

**Related Concepts:**
→ Introduction to Kinematics
→ Inverse Kinematics

📖 *Robot Kinematics and Motion* → Forward Kinematics
```
